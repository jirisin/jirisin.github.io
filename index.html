How I work

Research matched to the problem. Sometimes that's continuous discovery — 2–4 interviews a week, ~200 hours before a launch. Sometimes a focused study answers the question faster. Either way, I test before development, even when I'm confident — especially when I'm confident.

I build my own evidence. I set up the full analytics stack (Google Tag Manager, GA, Looker Studio) on the products I design — defining what to measure and why, not just reading dashboards.

I think product, not just screens. Acting Product Owner for 4 months. I've shaped roadmaps with Opportunity Solution Trees — and sometimes the right solution isn't a feature. A welcome email I designed for new developers scored 4.7/5 and solved an onboarding problem no UI could.

I invest in the design community around me. In a ~70-person UX organisation I ran a pairing programme connecting lone designers across teams, facilitated design thinking workshops for ~10 groups of non-UX colleagues, co-ran a UX workshop for Product Owners with a waiting list to attend, and mentored a junior designer.
